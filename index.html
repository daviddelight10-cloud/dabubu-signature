The House

Tailoring is our language — fit is how we speak it

From owambe gowns to matching mother-and-son sets, every piece leaving the workroom is drafted to your own measurements, not a size chart. We work in aso-oke, lace, ankara and hand-beaded fabric, pairing traditional Nigerian craftsmanship with a modern, editorial finish.

What we put on the table

Four ways to work with the atelier — each fitted, finished and delivered by hand.

01

Aso-Ebi & Occasion Wear

Statement gowns and fitted silhouettes for weddings, owambe and social events, built around your gele and accessories.

02

Aso-Oke & Beadwork

03

Mother & Child Matching Sets

Coordinated outfits for you and your little one — from coral-bead traditional wear to clean tailored whites.

04

Everyday & Chic Tailoring

How an Order Runs

From first sketch to final fitting

1

Consultation

Send your event date, fabric and inspiration photos on WhatsApp — we sketch a look around it.

2

Measurements

In-studio or guided self-measurement for clients outside Lagos, recorded to your personal file.

3

Cut & Construct

Your piece is drafted, cut and hand-finished, with a fitting scheduled midway for gowns and sets.

4

Delivery

Final press, packaging and delivery — timed so it's ready well ahead of your event date.

The Atelier

Built by hand, worn with confidence

Dabubu Signature started with a single sewing machine and a promise: clothes should fit the person, not the other way round. Today the atelier dresses clients for weddings, owambe, church and everyday occasions — every piece still passing through the same hands that started it all.

Whatever the fabric — aso-oke, lace, ankara or bead — we treat each order as a one-of-one.
